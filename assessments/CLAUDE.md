# Assessments

Quarterly assessment combining three elements. Takes 15-20 minutes.

---

## Format

Each quarterly file lives in `quarterly/` named `YYYY-QN.md`.

### 1. Happiness score (1-5)

Gut-check number. Not derived from the wheel — captures overall felt sense that individual pillar scores might miss.

### 2. Wheel of Life

Scored 1-10 on current satisfaction (not importance or achievement). The shape of the wheel matters more than the total — balance over raw score.

Customise these pillars to reflect your life. 6-8 pillars is the sweet spot.

| Pillar        | What it covers                                    |
| ------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| Health        | Sleep, energy, physical condition, exercise        |
| Work / Career | Professional development, job satisfaction, income |
| Relationships | Romantic life, dating                              |
| Family        | Close family relationships, care responsibilities  |
| Friends       | Friendships, social connections                    |
| Finances      | Financial health, savings, debt                    |
| Fun / Leisure | Hobbies, relaxation, enjoyment                     |
| Growth        | Learning, personal development, spirituality       |

**Guidelines:**
- Keep pillars stable for at least 2-3 cycles before revising
- If a pillar feels too broad, split it (e.g. "Family" into "Parents" and "Siblings")
- If a pillar never changes, it might not need tracking — drop it
- Your pillars should reflect your life, not a generic ideal

### 3. Reflection

Three questions with prompts to jog memory. Don't answer every sub-question — just use them as nudges.

Draws on Michael Bungay Stanier's coaching questions (_The Coaching Habit_) and CGP Grey's quarterly theme idea (_The Theme System_).

- **What shifted?** — what's better or different since last quarter?
  - Any wins, even small ones?
  - Did anything surprise you — something that improved without trying?
  - What did you actually _do_ differently vs just think about?

- **What's stuck?** — what hasn't moved despite wanting it to?
  - What are you avoiding? Why?
  - Is something stuck because it's genuinely hard, or because it's not actually a priority?
  - What would you be embarrassed to still be saying next quarter?

- **Where to focus next?** — pick 1-2 areas, not more.
  - If this quarter had a theme, what would it be? (e.g. "Season of Health", "Season of Order")
  - What's the one thing that, if it shifted, would move other things too?
  - What will you _stop_ paying attention to, to make room?

---

## Rules

- Assess quarterly (Jan, Apr, Jul, Oct)
- The conversation about the scores is where the value lives — not the form
- Don't turn this into planning. Planning happens in weekly reviews and projects.
