# Purpose & Principles

## What this is

Forte's pyramid places Purpose & Principles above PARA. PARA covers Projects and Areas — the actionable layers. Above them sit Goals, Vision, and at the top, Purpose & Principles — the stable, slow-changing layer that defines *why* those projects and areas matter.

```

Goals
---------------------
Areas                  <- PARA
Projects               <- PARA
```

This exercise builds the Purpose & Principles layer through two parts:

1. **Values questions** — reflective questions that surface who {{YOUR_NAME}} is, what matters, and how they operate. Answers get distilled into a values profile.
2. **12 Favorite Problems** — Feynman's concept, referenced by Forte. Open questions {{YOUR_NAME}} carries around that filter what's worth paying attention to and connect dots across domains. Professional and personal, big and small.

Forte provides the pyramid structure and says this layer should exist above PARA, but doesn't prescribe a specific exercise for building it. The values questions below are a starting point — edit, add, or remove questions to fit. The 12 Favorite Problems follow Feynman's original approach.

---

## Part 1 — Values questions

Take any, skip any. Write as much or as little as feels right.

**1. What are you most proud of in your life so far?**

**2. When you imagine yourself at 40, living well — what does that look like? Not achievements, just the shape of daily life.**

**3. When you feel guilty about relaxing, what's the voice actually saying? And whose voice is it?**

**4. What would you never compromise on, even if it made life harder?**

**5. When do you feel most like yourself?**

**6. What does doing good for your community look like concretely? What does "good" mean to you?**

**7. What do you respect most in other people?**

**8. Is there anything you currently spend time on that you know doesn't matter to you but you do anyway? Why?**

**9. What would you be most disappointed to look back and realise you never did?**

**10. How do you want to show up for the people closest to you? And where's the gap between that and how you actually show up?**

**11. What does doing good work feel like to you? When have you felt it?**

## Part 2 — 12 Favorite Problems

Feynman kept ~12 open questions constantly present in his mind. Whenever he encountered new information, he tested it against the list. Most connections won't materialise, but occasional matches generate unexpected breakthroughs.

Guidelines:
- Professional and personal — humbler problems count equally
- Formulate as open questions: "How can I...?", "What does it look like to...?", "What would have to be true for...?"
- Don't force all 12 in one sitting — let them emerge naturally over days or weeks
- "The worthwhile problems are the ones you can really solve or help solve" — Feynman
